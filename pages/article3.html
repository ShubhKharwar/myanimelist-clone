'Boukyaku Battery' Receives Second Season

by DatRandomDude

Yesterday, 10:05 PM | 30 Comments

The official website for the television anime adaptation of Eco Mikawa's Boukyaku Battery (Oblivion Battery) manga announced a second season on Sunday.

The first season, produced by MAPPA, aired in 12 episodes in Spring 2024. Crunchyroll simulcast the anime with English subtitles. The studio also produced an original net anime episode for the 2020 Jump Festa event.

Mikawa began serializing the sports manga on the Shounen Jump+ website in April 2018. Shueisha published the 19th volume on September 4, with the 20th volume scheduled to be released on December 4.

Synopsis
Haruka Kiyomine and Kei Kaname were once recognized as the most formidable pitcher-catcher duo in middle school baseball. Known as the "General," Kaname paired his expert strategies with Kiyomine's explosive pitches, crushing the dreams of many starry-eyed players who hoped to one day make it to the big leagues.

High school freshman Tarou Yamada is one such former player. Having lost all interest in baseball, Kaname dragged a disappointed Kiyomine to Kotesashi simply because the school was close to where they live.

As Kotesashi forms a fledgling baseball club, Yamada meets other players he once admired who also left baseball behind. While they might be unwilling now, Yamada believes that if he can convince such talented athletes to start playing again, it might be possible to achieve their dreams and win the national championship‚Äîas long as they can put up with Kaname's incessant joking.
